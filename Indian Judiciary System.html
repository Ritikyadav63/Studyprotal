Judiciary in India: Supreme Court, High Courts, and District Courts

The Indian Judiciary is an independent and integrated system consisting of the Supreme Court at the top, followed by **High Courts in the states and District/Subordinate Courts at the local level. It is governed by Part V (Articles 124–147) and Part VI (Articles 214–237) of the Constitution.

1. Supreme Court of India

  • Articles: 124–147
  • Composition: Chief Justice of India (CJI) and up to 33 other judges.
  • Tenure: Judges retire at 65 years.
  • Headquarters: New Delhi

Jurisdiction and Powers

  • Original Jurisdiction (Article 131): Disputes between Union and States.
  • Writ Jurisdiction (Article 32): Protection of Fundamental Rights.
  • Appellate Jurisdiction (Articles 132–136): Civil, criminal, and constitutional appeals.
  • Advisory Jurisdiction (Article 143): President may seek advice.
  • Judicial Review: Can declare laws unconstitutional.
  • Guardian of Constitution and Fundamental Rights.

2. High Courts

  • Articles: 214–231
  • One High Court per state or for multiple states/UTs.
  • Composition: Chief Justice and other judges appointed by the President.
  • Retirement Age: 62 years

Powers and Functions

  • Original Jurisdiction: On matters related to state laws.
  • Writ Jurisdiction (Article 226): Can issue writs for Fundamental and Legal Rights.
  • Appellate Jurisdiction: Hears appeals from lower courts.
  • Supervisory Jurisdiction: Supervises district courts under its territory.

3. District and Subordinate Courts

  • Articles: 233–237
  • Established by: State Governments
  • Presided by: District Judge (in civil matters), Sessions Judge (in criminal matters)
  • Other Courts: Magistrate courts, family courts, labour courts, etc.

Powers and Functions

  • Handles civil and criminal cases at the district level.
  • Appeals can be made to the High Court.
  • Important for local justice delivery and public access to courts.

Conclusion

The Judiciary ensures justice, interprets laws, protects rights, and upholds the Constitution. Its independence is a cornerstone of India's democratic system.

Salient Features of the Indian Constitution

1. Lengthiest Written Constitution

The Indian Constitution is the longest written constitution in the world. It originally had 395 Articles, 22 Parts, and 8 Schedules. Today, it contains 470 Articles, 25 Parts, and 12 Schedules due to amendments.

2. Blend of Rigidity and Flexibility

The Constitution can be amended by a special majority (rigid) or a simple majority (flexible), depending on the nature of the amendment. This balance ensures both stability and adaptability.

3. Federal System with Unitary Bias

India has a quasi-federal system, where powers are divided between the Centre and the States. However, the Centre has overriding powers, especially during emergencies, making it unitary in nature.

4. Parliamentary Form of Government

The President is the constitutional head, while the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers exercise real power. This system ensures collective responsibility and accountability to the Parliament.

5. Fundamental Rights

Part III of the Constitution guarantees six fundamental rights to all citizens, such as the Right to Equality, Right to Freedom, and Right to Constitutional Remedies, promoting liberty and justice.

6. Directive Principles of State Policy

Enshrined in Part IV, these are non-justiciable guidelines to promote social and economic democracy. Inspired by the Irish Constitution, they aim to build a welfare state.

7. Secular State

India is a secular state with no official religion. The State treats all religions equally and provides freedom of religion to all citizens under Article 25 to 28.

8. Single Citizenship

Unlike federal countries like the USA, India provides single citizenship to all its citizens, ensuring national unity and integrity.

9. Independent Judiciary

The judiciary is free from executive control. The Supreme Court and High Courts have the power of judicial review to protect citizens’ rights and uphold the Constitution.

10. Universal Adult Franchise

Every citizen of India who is 18 years or older has the right to vote, without discrimination based on caste, religion, or gender.

11. Emergency Provisions

The Constitution provides for National, State, and Financial Emergencies under Articles 352, 356, and 360, allowing the Centre to assume more powers in times of crisis.

12. Special Provisions for Minorities and Backward Classes

The Constitution provides reservations in education, employment, and legislatures to uplift socially and educationally backward groups and protect minorities.

Conclusion

The Indian Constitution is a unique document that reflects India's diversity, democratic ethos, and commitment to justice and equality. Its salient features make it dynamic and responsive to the nation's evolving needs.

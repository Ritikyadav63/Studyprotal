Union Executive

The Union Executive is the central governing authority in India. It consists of the President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, and the Council of Ministers. Their powers and responsibilities are defined under Part V of the Constitution (Articles 52 to 78).

1. President of India

  • Article 52: Establishes the office of the President.
  • Article 53: Vests the executive power of the Union in the President.
  • Article 60: Oath of office - to preserve, protect and defend the Constitution.
  • Article 72: Power to grant pardons, reprieves, etc.
  • Article 356: Power to impose President’s Rule in states.

Presidential Powers:

  • Executive: Appoints the Prime Minister, Governors, Judges of Supreme and High Courts, etc.
  • Legislative: Summons and prorogues Parliament, can dissolve Lok Sabha.
  • Judicial: Grants pardons, reprieves, commutations.
  • Emergency: Can declare National, State, and Financial Emergencies.

2. Vice-President of India

  • Article 63: Establishes the office of Vice-President.
  • Article 64: Ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.
  • Article 65: Acts as President in his absence.
  • Article 69: Oath of office - to discharge functions faithfully and uphold Constitution.

Vice-President's Powers:

  • Presides over the Rajya Sabha (Upper House).
  • Acts as President when the office is vacant or President is unable to discharge duties.

3. Prime Minister of India

  • Article 74: PM heads the Council of Ministers to aid and advise the President.
  • Article 75: PM is appointed by the President; ministers hold office during the pleasure of the President.
  • Third Schedule: Oath to bear true faith to the Constitution and perform duties faithfully.

Prime Minister's Powers:

  • Leader of the Union Cabinet and government.
  • Advises the President on appointments and policy matters.
  • Coordinates work of various ministries and departments.
  • Acts as chief spokesperson of the government in Parliament.

4. Council of Ministers

  • Article 74: Council of Ministers to aid and advise the President.
  • Article 75(3): Collective responsibility of the Council to Lok Sabha.
  • Third Schedule: Ministers take oath of office and secrecy.

Council's Powers:

  • Formulates and implements national policies and legislation.
  • Prepares the Union Budget and handles administrative functions.
  • Ministers handle specific departments and ministries (e.g., Finance, Defence, Home).

Conclusion

The Union Executive is the core of India's governance. While the President is the constitutional head, the Prime Minister and Council of Ministers exercise actual executive powers, ensuring democratic accountability. Oaths bind them to constitutional principles and public service.

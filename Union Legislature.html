Union Legislature: Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha

The Union Legislature, also known as the *Parliament of India, is a bicameral body comprising the **Rajya Sabha (Council of States)* and the *Lok Sabha (House of the People). It is governed by **Part V of the Constitution (Articles 79–122)*.

Structure of the Indian Parliament

  • Article 79: Parliament consists of the President, Rajya Sabha, and Lok Sabha.
  • Article 80: Composition of Rajya Sabha.
  • Article 81: Composition of Lok Sabha.
  • Article 122: Courts cannot question proceedings of Parliament.

Rajya Sabha (Council of States)

  • Total Strength: Maximum 250 members.
  • Current Strength: Around 245 members.
  • Tenure: Permanent body; one-third members retire every 2 years.
  • Presiding Officer: Vice President of India (ex-officio Chairman).

Rajya Sabha Powers and Functions

  • Participates in the law-making process.
  • Can introduce and pass all bills except money bills.
  • Approves constitutional amendments.
  • Article 249: Can authorize Parliament to make laws on State List subjects in national interest.
  • Article 312: Can create All India Services.

Lok Sabha (House of the People)

  • Total Strength: Maximum 552 members.
  • Current Strength: 543 elected members.
  • Tenure: 5 years unless dissolved earlier.
  • Presiding Officer: Speaker of Lok Sabha.

Lok Sabha Powers and Functions

  • Primary law-making body; introduces and passes bills including money bills.
  • Article 110: Defines money bills (can be introduced only in Lok Sabha).
  • Controls the executive through motions, questions, and discussions.
  • Votes on demands for grants and passes the Union Budget.
  • Article 75(3): Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok Sabha.

Role of the President in Parliament

  • Article 86: Right to address and send messages to Parliament.
  • Article 111: Assent to bills passed by Parliament.
  • Article 123: Power to issue ordinances when Parliament is not in session.

Conclusion

The Union Legislature plays a vital role in the functioning of Indian democracy. While the Rajya Sabha represents the states and serves as a revising chamber, the Lok Sabha is directly elected by the people and holds greater power in financial and executive matters.
